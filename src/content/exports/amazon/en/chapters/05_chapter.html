Advanced Techniques for Pros

From basic level to mastery

Once you've passed the beginner stage, pétanque is no longer just about placing a ball near the jack, but about solving difficult situations and mastering advanced techniques.

Chapter 5 – Improve Your Pointing

Advanced Placement Techniques

An effective method for advanced training is to create obstacles. Place three balls around the jack so that it's invisible, and practice placing your ball as close as possible without having to shoot. This forces you to find new landing zones (données) and develop a heightened sensitivity for backspin.

Training Exercise:

Place bricks or balls in a circle around the previous position and practice lobbing over the obstacle to make the ball stop near the jack. By varying the distance between 6 and 10 meters and gradually moving the obstacles, you work on precision at different distances.

Three Types of Pointing Throws

Throw Type Use
Rolling For smooth surfaces - the ball rolls on a flat trajectory
Half-lob For medium-distance throws on varied terrain
High lob For long throws on difficult terrain - the ball lands softly

Adapting Your Throw to Surface and Weather

  • Soft gravel court: The ball sinks and stops quickly; a flat roll is often safest here
  • Hard or rocky court: The ball bounces easily and can deflect; opt for a high lob with backspin
  • Sloped court: Throw the ball uphill to prevent it from rolling too far
  • Rain: The ground becomes heavier and balls stop faster
  • Dry weather: Increases bounce

Mental Training – Focus and Routines

At elite level, mental strength is often decisive. Set concrete training goals for each season and each session to maintain your focus.

Before throwing:

  1. Take a few deep breaths
  2. Visualize the throw's trajectory
  3. See the ball land where you want it
  4. "Turn off" conscious control during the throw itself

Training Sessions for Advanced Pointing

To take your pointing to the next level, you need to train with precision and measurable goals:

Exercise 1: The Obstacle Challenge

Setup: Place 3-6 balls or bricks around the jack so it's not directly visible. Practice lobbing over the obstacles and landing near the jack.

Goals:

  • Intermediate: 4/10 throws within 50 cm of the jack
  • Advanced: 6/10 throws within 30 cm of the jack
  • Expert: 3/10 throws closer than all obstacles

Log: Number of hits, which throw trajectory (low/medium/high) worked best

Exercise 2: Different Surfaces - Adaptability

Setup: Train on 3 different surfaces the same day (e.g. gravel, asphalt, grass). Throw 10 balls per surface.

Goals:

  • Identify which throw type (roll/half-lob/high lob) works best per surface
  • Hit within 50 cm: at least 5/10 on each surface

Log: Surface, best throw type, hit percentage, what needs adjustment

Exercise 3: Backspin Control

Setup: On hard surface, throw 10 balls focusing on maximum backspin. Measure how far the ball rolls after landing.

Goals:

  • Intermediate: Ball rolls <1 meter after landing on a 7m throw
  • Advanced: Ball rolls <50 cm after landing
  • Expert: Ball "dies" within 30 cm of landing point

Log: Roll distance per throw, wrist technique that worked best
