Equipment – Choose the Right Boules

Weight, size, material and maintenance

Weight, Size and Material – What Suits You?

Competition-approved boules must be hollow metal balls with a diameter between 70.5 and 80 mm and weight between 650 and 800 grams. The choice of size and weight is influenced by your hand size, role in the team and playing style:

Hand Size

A smaller hand is comfortable with a ball around 71–73 mm in diameter, while larger hands can choose 74–78 mm. The ball should feel comfortable but not too large; you should be able to grip it without straining your hand.

Weight

Lighter boules (650–690 g):

  • Provide better control and require less force
  • Good for pointers
  • Easier to control for beginners

Heavier boules (710–800 g):

  • Hold their line better when shooting
  • Require more strength
  • Provide more power in shots

Hardness

Softer boules ("tendre") absorb impacts and stop quickly on contact – ideal for shooters (tireurs) who want to make carreau. Harder boules ("dure") roll more predictably and give better control – excellent for pointers who need precision in the roll.

The Difference Between Competition and Recreational Boules

Recreational Boules:

  • Usually filled with sand or gravel
  • Have a chrome-plated surface
  • Come in a single size and weight
  • Cheap but do not meet competition regulations

Competition Boules:

  • Carefully manufactured, hollow machined
  • Meet FIPJP specifications
  • Available in many combinations of diameters, weights, hardnesses and groove patterns
  • More expensive but offer better balance and longevity

As a beginner, you can start with a cheaper recreational set to try the game, but invest in a set of competition boules when you feel you want to develop. A good ball that fits your hand provides increased precision and better feel.

Special junior sizes are available for children.

Maintenance and Cleaning of Boules

Metal balls rust if not cared for. After each game:

Maintenance Routine:

  1. Wash the balls with warm water and a little dish soap to remove dust and dirt. Dry thoroughly with a clean cloth.
  2. Lightly oil with an oiled cloth or maintenance spray – the oil forms a thin protective film that prevents oxidation.
  3. Store the balls in a dry and stable environment in a suitable bag or box. Avoid damp basements.

If Rust Occurs:

You can scrub the ball with a steel brush or let it sit in white wine vinegar which dissolves the rust, then rinse and dry. After cleaning, the balls should be oiled again for protection.

Important: With regular maintenance, your boules will last for many years. Treat them well and they will serve you well on the court!
