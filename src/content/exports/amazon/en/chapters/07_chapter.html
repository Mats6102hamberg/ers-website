Team Play â€“ Communication and Roles

The Three Roles in Triples

In triples, roles are often divided among three specialists. Each role has its specific responsibility and together they form a strong unit:

1. Pointeur (Pointer)

Main Task: To place their ball as close to the jack as possible. This is called "pointing" or "placing" the ball.

  • Focus: Precision, finesse and terrain observation
  • Goal: Create points for the team through accurate placements
  • Usually plays: First in the end
  • Characteristics: Calm, patient and has good feel for surfaces

2. Milieu (Middle Player/All-rounder)

Main Task: To be versatile â€“ a player who can do both the pointeur job (placing) and tireur job (shooting).

  • Focus: Adaptability and strategic overview
  • Goal: Act as link between pointeur and tireur and step in where needed most
  • Usually plays: In the middle of the end
  • Characteristics: Flexible, decisive and has good game understanding

3. Tireur (Shooter)

Main Task: To shoot (hit) the opponent's balls to knock them away from the jack and thus eliminate their point threat.

  • Focus: Strength, precision and concentration
  • Goal: Clear dangerous opponent balls through accurate shots
  • Usually plays: Last in the end
  • Specialty: "Shooting on iron" (tirer au fer) or making a "carreau" (where your own ball stays in place of the knocked-away ball)
  • Characteristics: Confident, focused and handles pressure

Communication is Key

Summary: The team's success depends on the pointeur creating points, the tireur destroying the opponent's points and the milieu adapting and supporting both roles.

To succeed, the team must act as a unit. Communicate clearly before each throw:

  • Is the goal to win the points?
  • Should we block the opponent?
  • Should we eliminate a dangerous ball?

Important: Never let two players throw without consulting each other â€“ the game is quickly lost when communication stops.

Team Tactics in Practice

  • Early in the match: Establish your playing style and test opponent's weaknesses
  • When leading: Play safely and avoid unnecessary risks
  • When behind: Dare to take risks and try to change the game's dynamics
  • At end of match: Stay calm and follow your game plan
